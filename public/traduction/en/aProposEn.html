Prostheses, since when has it existed?

The first prostheses are mentioned in a sacred Hindu book called Visphala and which represents a woman with an iron prosthesis.

Prosthetics before 1900

Capua's leg

The Leg of Capua is a prosthesis from the 3rd century BC. Discovered in 1885 in a tomb in Capua, Italy. It is one of the oldest known prostheses. The original having been destroyed by an air raid during World War II, a copy is kept at the Science Museum in London. The object is made of wood and covered with bronze. Its usefulness would have been more aesthetic than functional.

Ambroise Paré and the fifteenth century

Ambroise Paré is one of the greatest practitioners of the Renaissance, so great that he was called the father of modern surgery. He will be the surgeon of princes and kings, of Henri II and his descendants: François II, Charles IX and Henri III. His motto was: "Labor improbus omnia vincit" ("Hard work overcomes everything")

Ambroise Paré designed during the era of gunpowder (which had caused more and more amputations) sophisticated and mechanized prostheses intended for the wealthy.

American Civil War and Anesthesia

During the American Civil War there was a great advance in the world of prostheses because the anesthesia allowed a longer and less painful operation in the patient.

In 1950, the invention of the rubber foot without articulation

The SACH foot is invented in the United States. It is a rubber foot and without articulation. It is very resistant but is especially suitable for people who are not very active.

In 1960, the invention of the articulated knee

It was in 1960 in England that the first total knee replacement was placed by an English surgeon named John Charnley.

In 1970, the hydraulic knee was invented

It has the characteristic of projecting the leg forward using a hydraulic cylinder, that is to say a cylinder which contains a liquid propelled by valves. The hydraulic unit helps make the throwing movement of the leg more natural.

In 1974 Mittelmeier invented a ceramic hip prosthesis.

It occupies the function of the joint, the rod being pressed into the thigh bone and the head and the cup serving for the mobility of the joint.

In 1997, prostheses and microprocessors were peer

In 1997, a microprocessor-controlled leg prosthesis was installed. It is fitted with sensors and its movements can be adjusted in real time. In its current version, it allows activities such as cycling.
